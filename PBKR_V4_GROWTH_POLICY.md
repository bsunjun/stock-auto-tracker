# PBKR v4 — Growth Policy

> Educational doctrine. Not advice. Not a signal source.

---

## 1. Role of Growth in v4

Growth is a **gate**, not a thesis. A name either clears the gate (eligible)
or it does not (ineligible). Eligibility never sets size, never sets entry
timing, and never overrides tape evidence.

The point of the gate is to filter out structurally stagnant names whose
price action, even when constructive, tends to fade because there is no
underlying business momentum to support extension.

---

## 2. The Three Growth Floors

A name is eligible if it clears **at least two of three** floors over the
trailing four reported quarters:

| Floor | Operational definition |
|---|---|
| Revenue | Trailing-4Q revenue YoY growth >= floor R% |
| Operating profit | Trailing-4Q operating profit YoY growth >= floor O%, or operating-margin expansion of >= M ppt |
| Cash | Trailing-4Q operating cash flow positive and growing, or net cash position increasing |

Floor values are calibrated per market regime and stage; defaults are
maintained in the operating notebook, not in this document, so that
calibration drift does not require a doctrine revision.

Forecasts and consensus revisions are explicitly **excluded** from the gate.
Only reported numbers count.

---

## 3. Anti-Goodhart Provisions

- One-off gains, asset disposals, and reversal of provisions are stripped
  before the gate is evaluated.
- A single blowout quarter does not lift a name through the gate; the
  trailing-four-quarter window is intentional.
- Aggressive accounting flags (receivables growing materially faster than
  revenue, inventory build outpacing COGS, capitalized R&D ratio rising)
  demote a name to a *watch* status even if the gate is cleared.

---

## 4. Disqualifications (Independent of the Gate)

A name is ineligible regardless of growth if any of the following holds:

- Going-concern qualification or modified audit opinion.
- Outstanding restatement or material accounting investigation.
- Liquidity below the floor defined in
  `PBKR_V4_KOREAN_MARKET_ADAPTATION.md`.
- Designated as administrative-issue or investment-attention by the
  exchange (see Korean-market doc).
- Pending capital event (rights offering, large CB issuance) within a
  short window before/after the candidate entry, where dilution risk
  is non-trivial.

---

## 5. Re-Entry After Failure

A name that fails the gate cannot be re-evaluated for eligibility until a
new quarterly report is published. Tape strength alone does not unlock the
gate. This is intentional: the gate exists precisely to prevent tape
enthusiasm from rationalizing structurally weak businesses.

---

## 6. Growth-Adjacent Tailwinds (Soft Inputs)

These are **not** part of the gate but inform watchlist priority:

- Group-level momentum in adjacent listed peers.
- Order-book or backlog disclosures.
- Capacity-utilization disclosures, where reported.
- Industry-level shipment or pricing data published by official sources.

These soft inputs change watchlist *ranking*, never *eligibility* and never
*size*.

---

## 7. Documentation Requirement

For each eligible name, the watchlist record must contain:

1. The two-of-three floors cleared, with the trailing-4Q numbers.
2. The disqualification checks performed and their result.
3. Any soft-input notes that affected watchlist ranking.
4. The next quarterly report date, after which eligibility is re-evaluated.

Eligibility decisions without this record are non-doctrinal.
